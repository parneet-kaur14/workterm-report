Introduction

This website provides a glimpse into my first co-op work term, completed from May to August 2025 at the Experiential Learning Hub (EL Hub), University of Guelph as a Systems Support Technician.

What I hope readers will take away is how this experience turned into a pool of learning opportunities that helped me grow both professionally and academically.

Throughout this term, I supported the Experience Guelph platform, which connects students, employers, and faculty through job postings and recruitment processes. My responsibilities included managing support tickets, resolving student and employer inquiries, and escalating technical issues to Computing & Communications Services (CCS), the University’s central IT department. I also assisted staff in person with troubleshooting requests and contributed to creating student user guides, which improved the clarity and accessibility of resources.

This report will share my experiences, goals, and reflections from this co-op, showing how my first work term became a foundation for both professional growth and academic development.

Employer

The Experiential Learning Hub (EL Hub) at the University of Guelph is the central department that connects students, employers, and faculty through career and co-op opportunities. The EL Hub manages a wide range of experiential learning programs, including Co-operative Education, Career Education, and Experience Guelph.

Located in the University Centre, the EL Hub serves thousands of students each year by providing resources, workshops, and job opportunities that help students prepare for their careers. A key part of this department is Experience Guelph, the online recruitment platform where employers post opportunities and students apply for jobs.

During my work term, I supported the EL Hub by helping to maintain the Experience Guelph platform and assisting staff, students, and employers with technical and administrative inquiries.

Job Description

As a Systems Support Technician at the Experiential Learning Hub during Summer 2025, my role combined technical troubleshooting with system support for the Experience Guelph platform and the day-to-day IT needs of staff.

Overview of my role:

EGExperience Guelph Ticket Management
Investigating and resolving support tickets, responding to student and employer inquiries, and escalating complex issues to Computing & Communications Services (CCS) when required.

ITStaff IT Support
Troubleshooting laptops, phones (including Jabber), boardroom technology, and other in-office equipment.

DOCDocumentation
Creating student user guides, tutorials, and tip sheets to improve clarity and accessibility.

1A unique start to the role:

I had expected my co-op to begin with formal training, but due to circumstances, I was brought into the team’s work right away. While this was an unexpected start, it quickly taught me the importance of being adaptable. Instead of observing, I was contributing from day one, which helped me build confidence and reinforced that flexibility and teamwork are just as critical as technical knowledge in a real workplace.

2Interesting aspect of my job:

What stood out most to me was how my perspective shifted. In the past, I was on the other side of Experience Guelph — sending emails to ask for help. This summer, I became the person answering those very queries. Experiencing the full cycle gave me a deeper appreciation for the systems and the people who keep them running smoothly.

Skills I used and developed:

Problem Solving Troubleshooting Organization Communication Documentation

This position required skills in problem solving, technical troubleshooting, organization, and communication. Some of these I first learned in class (like structured problem solving, documentation, and databases), while others came from previous jobs where I practiced customer service and teamwork. On this co-op, I strengthened those foundations and developed new abilities through hands-on experience — especially learning how to analyze problems, escalate effectively, and communicate solutions clearly to both technical and non-technical users.

Overall, the role allowed me to combine classroom knowledge with workplace learning, while gaining confidence in handling real technical challenges.

Conclusion

This co-op work term showed me that learning in class and applying knowledge in real life are two very different experiences. While courses build a foundation, the workplace adds new challenges that require adaptability, problem solving, and collaboration every day.

There were times when I was not at my best energy or faced tasks that seemed overwhelming, but the support of my team and the drive to keep learning helped me push through. Each day brought something new — whether it was solving a technical problem, assisting a staff member, or improving documentation — and these experiences helped me grow both professionally and personally.

If there is one message I hope readers take from this report, it is that co-op is not only about building technical skills, but also about building confidence, resilience, and the ability to work together. These lessons will carry forward into my next co-op and beyond, shaping the way I approach future opportunities.
